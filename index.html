The 5 rules

The contract you made with yourself.

1
Homework before curiosity — always. Submit first, explore after.
2
When you drift, park it — don't follow it. 5 seconds to write it down, then return.
3
One task per block. French time is not dev time. Dev time is not French time.
4
The transition breath is not optional. It is what separates finishing from drifting.
5
Ship something small every day in dev — even one line of real code counts. Curiosity without output is just entertainment.

Daily practices

Small habits that protect your focus.

⚓
Box breathing
4 min
Before every block. Inhale 4, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4. Clears mental fog before demanding work.
◎
Body scan
5 min
Mid-morning. Scan feet → legs → belly → shoulders → jaw. Soften each area. Kitchen work makes tension creep up without you noticing.
◈
Transition breath
2 min
Between every block. Stand up, 5 slow breaths, say: "That task is done. This is what's next." This is what separates finishing from drifting.
⇄
Spacing effect
every 75 min
Switch French modes every 60-75 minutes — grammar → listening → speaking → curiosity. Each switch is a rest for the others. Never one long block.
✎
Parking lot rule
5 sec
When curiosity pulls you — park it in 5 seconds, return immediately. Curiosity time comes after homework, not during.
✦
Focus word
every evening
Pick one French word for tomorrow. Say it once before your first block. Ancrage, concentration, patience — one word sets the tone for the day.
◑
The 4am rule
every morning
Your most controlled time is 4-7am. Protect it completely. No social media, no messages, no decisions — just the blocks.
